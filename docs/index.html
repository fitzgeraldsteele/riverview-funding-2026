Riverview School District
2026 Bonds and Levies Review

Election Day: February 10, 2026

Three measures are on the ballot for Riverview School District No. 407 voters in King County, Washington. Two are renewal levies that fund ongoing operations and technology. One is a new capital bond to replace Tolt Middle School and improve all district facilities. This guide presents the facts, data, and context so you can make an informed decision.

This is an independent informational resource. It is not affiliated with Riverview School District, any campaign, or any political organization. Data sourced from public records and news reporting. See all sources.

At a Glance

Three propositions, each voted on independently. Propositions 1 & 2 need a simple majority. Proposition 3 requires 60% approval.

              Enrollment & Growth

              District enrollment has declined ~9% from peak, but middle school enrollment is projected to grow 21% by 2028 due to new housing development in Duvall and Carnation.

              District-Wide Enrollment Trend

              Total district enrollment has declined ~9% since the 2018-19 peak. Kindergarten enrollment (180 actual vs. 208 budgeted in 2024-25) suggests the trend may continue short-term.

              Middle School Enrollment ‚Äî Current & Projected

              Despite district-wide declines, middle school enrollment is projected to grow 21% by 2028-29, driven by 380 new housing starts in Duvall and 200+ in Carnation.
